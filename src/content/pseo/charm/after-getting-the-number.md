Text her the same day or the next morning — inside 24 hours — with a message that does two things: reminds her exactly who you are, and gives her something easy and fun to reply to. The number is not the win; the first text deciding whether she's glad she gave it to you is the win.

## What's actually going on

A number exchanged in person (or moved off a dating app) has a short half-life. In the moment, she matched a face to a vibe and said yes. Twelve hours later, you're a contact named "Mike bar?" competing with everything else on her phone. Every hour you wait, the memory of *why* she gave you the number fades — which is why the "wait three days" rule is the single most reliable way to turn a warm number cold.

Your first text has one job: re-create the moment. Reference something specific from the conversation — the joke, the argument about pineapple pizza, the band she mentioned. Specificity proves you were actually listening, separates you from every generic "hey it's me from last night," and hands her an obvious thread to pull.

If the number came from an app rather than in person, the same logic applies — anchor to something from the profile or chat. Our guide to [dating app openers](/charm/blog/dating-app-openers/) covers that variant in depth.

## What NOT to send

- **"Hey it's Mike"** — and nothing else. You've given her a reply that requires her to do all the work.
- **"Hey, it was great meeting you last night! You seemed really cool and I'd love to get to know you better."** — polite, sincere, and completely flavorless. It could have been sent by anyone, to anyone.
- **A date request in text one.** "Drinks Thursday?" before any warm-up feels transactional. One or two exchanges first.
- **Anything at 2am.** Even if you just left the bar. Save the draft, send it at brunch time.
- **A paragraph.** Long first texts read as intense. Two lines max.

## Copy-ready texts

Swap in the real details — the specifics are the whole trick.

### Playful

> This is your formal notice that I looked it up and pineapple pizza was invented in Canada. You were wrong on multiple continents.

> Hey, it's [name] — the one who promised you the best taco recommendation of your life. I take this responsibility extremely seriously.

> Quick quality check: did I make the cut into your phone as [name], or am I "guy from Emma's party"?

### Direct

> Hey, it's [name] from last night. That conversation was the best part of my evening — glad you were bold enough to be at the snack table.

> [Name] here. I meant it about the bookstore — you'd lose your mind in there. Keeping it in my back pocket for a future field trip.

### Low-key

> Found the song we couldn't remember: [song]. It was bugging me the whole way home.

> It's [name] 🙂 That comedy special you mentioned — just started it. You have exactly one episode of credibility on the line.

> Saw this and immediately thought of your dog-in-a-raincoat story [photo/meme]

Notice none of these ask "how are you" and none of them ask her out yet. They all end somewhere she can jump in without effort.

## Timing

- **In person, evening exchange:** text the next morning or early afternoon. Next-day is the sweet spot — interested but not camped on your phone.
- **In person, daytime exchange:** same evening is perfect. "Made it home with zero further pineapple arguments" lands great six hours later.
- **From an app:** within a few hours of getting the number. Moving off-app is momentum; don't park it.
- **Her first reply comes fast and warm?** Match her pace. Two to four good exchanges, then propose the plan — a specific day and place, not "we should hang out sometime." If you tend to stall here, [how to ask someone out over text](/charm/blog/how-to-ask-someone-out-over-text/) has the exact phrasing.

## When to stop

If the first text gets no reply in 48 hours, send one — exactly one — light follow-up on a different topic. If that also dies, she gave the number in a moment that didn't survive the night. It happens; it costs you nothing but the thirty seconds you spent typing. Do not send a third message, and do not ask why she gave you the number "if she wasn't interested." Numbers are given easily; attention is the real signal.

## FAQ

**Should I text the same night I got her number?**
If you met during the day, yes. If you met at night, a short "glad I met you, more tomorrow" text is fine, but the real opener works better the next day.

**What if I can't remember anything specific from the conversation?**
Then use the meta angle: "In my defense, I remember your name, your laugh, and absolutely nothing about how we got onto the topic of pigeons." Owning the blur is its own callback.

**How long should the first exchange go before suggesting a date?**
A handful of back-and-forths across a day or two. Long enough to confirm the vibe, short enough that the thread doesn't become the relationship.

**She replied with one word. Bad sign?**
One dry reply is nothing — respond once more with something easy. A *pattern* of one-word replies is the signal; the [dry texter guide](/charm/blog/dry-texter/) covers how to read it.
