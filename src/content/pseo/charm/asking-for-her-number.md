Short answer: ask when the conversation has proof of life — real back-and-forth, a joke that landed, a plan forming — and ask plainly, with a reason attached. "This is too good for the app's terrible notifications. What's your number?" beats a week of hoping she offers it first.

## What's actually going on

The number ask is a smaller moment for her than it is for you. In your head it's a verdict on the whole conversation; in hers it's a logistics question — *is this guy worth letting one level closer to my actual life?* She's not grading the sentence you use; she's checking whether the vibe has earned it.

Which means the ask itself is rarely what fails — the *timing* is. Message four is too early to be a person to her; message four hundred means you've become a pen pal she'll never meet. App conversations have a shelf life: if a good chat doesn't move somewhere real (her number, a date, ideally both), it goes stale, and stale threads get archived without ceremony.

There's also the off-app version: you met at a party or through friends, and now you're texting on Instagram or in a group chat. Same logic. The move from "person in my DMs" to "person in my contacts" is the move from *audience* to *option*, and someone has to make it. If the conversation has energy, that someone is you.

One mindset correction before the scripts: the number is not the prize. The number is infrastructure. The prize is the date. The strongest asks bundle the two — "let's grab that drink, here's my number so we can actually plan it" — because a number attached to a plan converts, and a number collected for its own sake mostly just sits there.

## What NOT to send

- **"Can I have your number?"** — technically fine, emotionally flat. A bare permission request makes her do all the deciding with none of the momentum.
- **"We should text sometime"** — "sometime" is where plans go to die, and "we should" isn't an ask, it's a mood.
- **Asking in message three.** Before you've had one real exchange, the ask reads as a copy-paste you send everyone — because it usually is.
- **"I don't really use this app much…"** as the whole pitch. Everyone says it. If the app is the problem, offer the solution: a plan.
- **Asking twice in the same thread.** If she sidestepped it once, pushing again converts a "not yet" into a "now definitely not."

## Copy-ready texts

Send one. Match the tone to the thread you actually have, not the thread you wish you had.

### Playful

> This conversation has officially outgrown the app. I'm freeing it — what's your number?

> Proposal: we continue this somewhere my phone actually notifies me. I'm [number] — text me something weird.

### Direct

> I like talking to you and I'd rather do it off here. What's your number?

> Let's do that ramen place you mentioned — Thursday? Here's my number so we can actually plan it: [number].

> You seem like more fun than this app deserves. Give me your number and let's set up that drink.

### Low-key

> App's being glitchy on my end — here's my number in case it eats the thread: [number]

> I'm way better at texting than at whatever this interface is. [number] if you want to switch.

Notice that half of these *give* your number instead of requesting hers. That's deliberate: offering yours is the lower-pressure version of the same move — she decides on her own clock, and if she texts you, she's already invested. Requesting is slightly more confident; offering is easier to say yes to. Pick based on how warm the thread is.

## Timing

- **On the apps: after the first real exchange, before the tenth day.** Usually between message ten and thirty — enough that you're a person, not so much that you've become a hobby. Still warming the thread up? Our guide on [what to text after matching](/charm/what-to-text/after-matching/) covers that runway.
- **The best trigger is a plan, not a milestone.** The moment a date idea appears in the conversation — a place she mentioned, a thing you both want to try — that's your window. Numbers attached to plans get used.
- **Off the apps (met in person): within a day or two of the meeting**, while the memory is warm. "Great meeting you last night — I'm claiming the number your friend refused to give me" works at hour twenty, not at week three.
- **Ask at a human hour.** Evening beats 2am for the same reason it always does.
- **After you ask, let it breathe.** She may answer in minutes or in a day. Neither tells you much. What tells you something is *whether* she answers.

## When to stop

You get one clean ask per thread. If she gives you the number — great, use it within a day, ideally to lock the plan; then see [what to text before the first date](/charm/what-to-text/before-first-date/) so the momentum survives until you meet.

If she deflects — "let's keep chatting here for now," "I don't give my number out this early" — that's not a rejection, it's pace-setting. Respect it: "Totally fair. Here works." Then keep being the person worth giving a number to — very often she'll offer it herself within the week, on her terms, which is the better outcome anyway.

If she ignores the ask and the thread goes quiet, treat it like any other silence: one light re-engagement on a fresh topic after a couple of days, and if that dies too, the answer arrived. Don't re-ask for the number in that text — that's asking her to reject you twice.

## FAQ

**How many messages before asking for her number on a dating app?**
Roughly ten to thirty messages across a few days — enough for one real exchange of substance. But the better trigger than counting is a plan: the moment a concrete date idea shows up, ask.

**Should I give my number or ask for hers?**
Both work. Asking for hers is slightly more confident; giving yours is lower-pressure and lets her opt in on her own clock. If the thread is warm, ask. If it's promising but tentative, offer.

**She said she'd rather keep talking on the app. Is that a rejection?**
No — it's pacing. Plenty of women keep early conversations on the app for safety and simplicity. Say "totally fair," keep the conversation good, and let her move it when she's ready. Pushing back is the only real way to lose here.

**She gave me her number but the texting went cold. What happened?**
The number was infrastructure, not commitment. If you collected it without attaching a plan, the thread often loses the app's built-in momentum and stalls. Fix it by sending a plan, not more small talk.

**Is it weird to ask for her number if we met in person, not on an app?**
It's the opposite of weird — it's the expected next move. Within a day or two of meeting, one warm, specific text ("Great meeting you at Sam's thing — I want to hear the rest of that story. Drinks this week?") does the whole job.
