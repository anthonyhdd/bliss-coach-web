How do you say...? in Spanish is **¿cómo se dice?** — arguably the most powerful phrase a learner can own, because it lets you build vocabulary in real time from any native speaker. Point at something, say *¿cómo se dice esto?* ("how do you say this?"), and you've turned the world into a free dictionary. Pair it with **¿cómo se llama?** ("what's it called?") and you can name anything you don't have a word for yet.

## The main way to say it

**¿Cómo se dice?** — pronounced **KOH-moh seh DEE-seh**.

The pronunciation details that matter:

- **Cómo** is two syllables, stress on the first: **KOH**-moh. The accent on *có* marks it as the question word "how."
- **Se** is a quick, unstressed "seh."
- **Dice** is **DEE**-seh, stress on the first syllable. In Mexican Spanish the *c* before *e* is an "s" sound, so it's DEE-**s**eh (never the Spain "th").

To ask for a specific word, tack it on:

- **¿Cómo se dice "apple" en español?** = how do you say "apple" in Spanish?
- **¿Cómo se dice esto?** = how do you say this? (holding something up)
- **¿Cómo se dice ___ en inglés?** = how do you say ___ in English? (asking the reverse)

The **se** here is impersonal — "how does *one* say it" — which is exactly the vague "you" English uses in "how do you say." You're not asking about a specific person.

## Variations

| Phrase | Meaning | Register |
|--------|---------|----------|
| ¿Cómo se dice? | How do you say (it)? | Universal |
| ¿Cómo se dice esto? | How do you say this? | Universal |
| ¿Cómo se dice ___ en español? | How do you say ___ in Spanish? | Universal |
| ¿Cómo se llama esto? | What's this called? | Everyday |
| ¿Cómo le dicen a esto? | What do they call this? (local word) | Casual, Mexican |
| ¿Cómo se escribe? | How do you spell/write it? | Useful follow-up |
| ¿Qué es esto en español? | What's this in Spanish? | Casual |
| ¿Existe una palabra para...? | Is there a word for...? | Conversational |

A very Mexican variant is **¿cómo le dicen a esto?** ("what do they call this?") — great for asking about regional or slang names for something, since Mexico has plenty of words that differ from "textbook" Spanish. And once you've got the word, the natural follow-up is **¿cómo se escribe?** ("how do you spell it?") so you can write it down and keep it.

## Hear it in context

**Learning a new word on the spot (learner-friendly):**

> — Oye, ¿cómo se dice "straw" en español?
> — Popote. Aquí en México le decimos popote.
> — ¿Popote? ¿Y cómo se escribe?
> — P-O-P-O-T-E. En España le dicen "pajita."

*Hey, how do you say "straw" in Spanish? — Popote. Here in Mexico we call it popote. — Popote? And how do you spell it? — P-O-P-O-T-E. In Spain they say "pajita."*

**Asking what something is called (everyday):**

> — ¿Cómo se llama esta fruta? Nunca la había visto.
> — Es un mamey. Está buenísimo, pruébalo.
> — ¿Mamey? Qué rico. ¿Cómo se dice en inglés?
> — Uf, no sé, creo que no tiene traducción.

*What's this fruit called? I'd never seen it. — It's a mamey. It's delicious, try it. — Mamey? So good. How do you say it in English? — Ugh, I don't know, I don't think it has a translation.*

## Common mistakes

- **Dropping the "se."** *¿Cómo dice?* means "how does he/she say it?" or "what did you say?" — not "how do you say." You need the impersonal *se*: *¿cómo se dice?*.
- **Confusing "dice" and "llama."** *¿Cómo se dice?* asks for the *word* ("how do you say it"). *¿Cómo se llama?* asks for the *name* of a thing or person ("what's it/he/she called"). Both are useful; don't blur them.
- **Using the Spain "th" for "dice."** In Mexico, *dice* is DEE-seh with an "s." The lisped DEE-theh is Castilian.
- **Forgetting "en español."** If it's not obvious which language you want, add it: *¿cómo se dice ___ en español?*. Otherwise a native might answer in the wrong direction.

## Mexico vs Spain notes

The phrase itself, *¿cómo se dice?*, is universal — it works identically everywhere.

- **Pronunciation:** Mexico says DEE-**s**eh; Spain says DEE-**th**eh (the *c* before *e* lisps). Both understand each other perfectly.
- **The words you'll learn differ:** ask *¿cómo se dice?* about the same object in Mexico and Spain and you may get different answers — *popote* (Mexico) vs *pajita* (Spain) for "straw," *carro* vs *coche* for "car." That's exactly why the phrase is so handy for travelers.
- **¿Cómo le dicen?** (with the *le*) is a Mexican/Latin American flavor for asking the local nickname of something; Spain leans on *¿cómo se llama?*.

## FAQ

**What's the difference between "¿cómo se dice?" and "¿cómo se llama?"**
*¿Cómo se dice?* asks how to *say* a word ("how do you say 'apple'"). *¿Cómo se llama?* asks the *name* of something or someone ("what's this called," "what's your name"). Use *dice* for translations, *llama* for names.

**Why is there a "se" in the phrase?**
The *se* makes it impersonal — "how does one say it" — matching English's vague "you" in "how do you say." Without it, *¿cómo dice?* changes the meaning.

**How do I ask how to spell the word after?**
Say *¿cómo se escribe?* ("how is it written / spelled"). It's the natural follow-up once you've heard a new word.

**How do I specify Spanish or English?**
Add *en español* or *en inglés*: *¿cómo se dice "table" en español?* or *¿cómo se dice "popote" en inglés?*.

**Is "dice" pronounced with a "th" like in Spain?**
Not in Mexico. Mexican Spanish uses an "s" sound: DEE-seh. The lisped "th" (DEE-theh) is Castilian Spanish from Spain.
