Let's eat in Spanish is **vamos a comer** — or, when the food is on the table and it's time to dig in, just the punchy **¡a comer!**. The two aren't quite the same: *vamos a comer* is "let's go eat" (heading to a meal, maybe out somewhere), while *¡a comer!* is the call to the table, the Spanish equivalent of "food's ready, come eat!" Both run on the verb *comer* ("to eat"), and both are everyday essentials.

## The main way to say it

**Vamos a comer** — pronounced **BAH-mohs ah koh-MEHR**.

The pronunciation details that matter:

- **Vamos** = "BAH-mohs." The Spanish *v* is a soft *b*, so it's BAH, not VAH. Stress on the first syllable.
- **Comer** = "koh-MEHR," stress on the *second* syllable, with a rolled-but-soft final *r*.
- The little **a** in the middle ("ah") is essential — *vamos **a** comer*, never *vamos comer*.

The two forms and when to use them:

- **Vamos a comer** = let's go eat / let's have a meal. Uses the *vamos a + infinitive* structure ("we're going to…"). Great for suggesting a meal out: *vamos a comer tacos*.
- **¡A comer!** = to the table! / let's eat! It's the shout that calls everyone to sit down when the food is served. Short, warm, and very domestic.

Note: in Mexico, **comer** by default means the midday meal (the big one), while *desayunar* is breakfast and *cenar* is dinner. *Vamos a comer* often implies lunch specifically.

At a Mexican home, the summons to eat is usually **¡ya está la comida!** ("food's ready!") followed by **¡a comer!** or **¡a la mesa!**. A quick, casual "shall we eat?" between friends is simply **¿comemos?** — one word does it.

Remember to match the meal verb: *comer* (lunch/eat), **cenar** (dinner), **desayunar** (breakfast). *Vamos a cenar* is "let's have dinner."

## Hear it in context

**Mom calling the family to the table (domestic):**

> — ¡Ya está la comida! ¡A comer!
> — Ya voy, mamá. ¿Qué hiciste?
> — Milanesas. Siéntense, se enfría.
> — ¡Provecho a todos!

*Food's ready! Let's eat! — Coming, mom. What did you make? — Breaded cutlets. Sit down, it's getting cold. — Enjoy, everyone!*

**Friends deciding to grab lunch (casual):**

> — Oye, ¿comemos? Ya es la una.
> — Va, vamos a comer. ¿Qué se te antoja?
> — Unos tacos al pastor, muero de hambre.
> — ¡Órale! Yo conozco un lugar buenísimo.

*Hey, shall we eat? It's already one. — Sure, let's go eat. What are you craving? — Some al pastor tacos, I'm starving. — Cool! I know a great place.*

## Common mistakes

- **"Vamos comer" (dropping the "a").** The structure requires the preposition: *vamos **a** comer*. English hides it ("let's go eat"), but Spanish never drops it.
- **Using "comer" for every meal.** In Mexico, *comer* leans toward lunch. For dinner use *cenar*, for breakfast *desayunar*. *Vamos a comer* at 8 p.m. can sound off; *vamos a cenar* fits.
- **Pronouncing "vamos" with an English "v."** It's a soft *b*: BAH-mohs. This small fix upgrades your whole accent.
- **Stressing "comer" on the first syllable.** It's koh-**MEHR**, stress on the second. "KOH-mer" sounds wrong.

## Mexico vs Spain notes

*Vamos a comer* and *¡a comer!* are universal — understood and used everywhere.

- The **meaning of "comer" shifts by region**: in Mexico and much of Latin America, *comer* implies the midday meal (lunch). In Spain, *comer* also means lunch specifically, so this one actually aligns — but note that in some countries *almorzar* is the more common word for "to have lunch."
- **¡A la mesa!** and **¡a comer!** are used in both Mexico and Spain as the table call.
- Pronunciation is regionally neutral here; nothing lisps.

## FAQ

**What's the difference between "vamos a comer" and "¡a comer!"?**
*Vamos a comer* means "let's go eat / let's have a meal" — often heading out. *¡A comer!* is the call to the table when food is served: "let's eat / come and eat!" One suggests, the other summons.

**Why do I need the "a" in "vamos a comer"?**
The *vamos a + infinitive* structure always keeps the *a*: *vamos **a** comer*, *vamos **a** ver*. English drops it ("let's go eat"), but Spanish requires it.

**Does "comer" mean lunch or just "to eat"?**
Both. Literally *comer* is "to eat," but in Mexico it commonly refers to the midday meal (lunch). For dinner say *cenar*, for breakfast *desayunar*.

**How do I say "shall we eat?" casually?**
Just *¿comemos?* — one word. Or *¿vamos a comer algo?* ("shall we go grab something to eat?").

**How do I call people to the table?**
*¡A comer!*, *¡a la mesa!*, or *¡ya está la comida!* ("food's ready!"). All are warm, everyday household calls.
