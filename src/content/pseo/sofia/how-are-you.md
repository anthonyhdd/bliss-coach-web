How are you in Spanish is **¿cómo estás?** — and the follow-up every learner needs is that Spanish has a formal twin, **¿cómo está?**, plus a whole family of casual cousins Mexicans actually use with friends: *¿qué onda?*, *¿qué tal?*, *¿cómo te va?* Pick the wrong register and nothing breaks — but pick the right one and people visibly warm up to you.

## The main way to say it

**¿Cómo estás?** — pronounced **KOH-moh ehs-TAHS**.

The essentials:

- **Stress lands on -TAS**: ehs-**TAHS**. That final written accent (*estás*) is your map.
- The ending is the whole grammar: **estás** (with -s) is the informal *tú* form for friends, family, kids, and peers. **Está** (no -s) is the formal *usted* form for elders, strangers, officials, and professional settings: *¿Cómo está (usted)?*
- Crucial verb choice: it's **estar**, not *ser*. *¿Cómo estás?* asks how you're doing right now. *¿Cómo eres?* — with *ser* — asks what you're like as a person ("describe yourself"). Mixing them up turns small talk into a personality interview. If the *ser/estar* split still feels fuzzy, our [ser vs. estar guide](/sofia/blog/ser-vs-estar/) untangles it.

In Mexico, greetings and "how are you" fuse into one move: *Hola, ¿cómo estás?* or *Buenos días, ¿cómo está?* Asking is expected — skipping it and going straight to business feels brusque.

## Variations

| Phrase | Meaning | Register |
|--------|---------|----------|
| ¿Cómo estás? | How are you? | Informal default |
| ¿Cómo está (usted)? | How are you? | Formal |
| ¿Qué tal? | How's it going? | Casual, safe almost anywhere |
| ¿Cómo te va? | How's it going for you? | Casual-friendly |
| ¿Cómo le va? | How's it going for you? | Formal but warm — very common in Mexico |
| ¿Cómo has estado? | How have you been? | For someone you haven't seen in a while |
| ¿Qué onda? | What's up? (lit. "what wave?") | Mexican slang — friends only |
| ¿Qué pasó? | What's up? (lit. "what happened?") | Mexican casual — nothing needs to have happened |
| ¿Qué hubo? / ¡Quiubo! | What's up? | Mexican casual, compressed in speech |
| ¿Todo bien? | All good? | Quick check-in |

**¿Qué onda?** deserves its own paragraph because it's *the* Mexican greeting: literally "what wave?", functionally "what's up?". Use it with friends and people your age — never to open with your girlfriend's grandmother or a visa officer. And know that in Mexico, *¿qué pasó?* is usually just a hello; if a friend fires it at you, they're not asking what went wrong.

**How to answer** — keep it short, positive, and bounce it back:

- *Bien, gracias, ¿y tú?* — Fine thanks, and you? (informal)
- *Muy bien, gracias, ¿y usted?* — Very well, and you? (formal)
- *Todo bien, ¿y tú qué?* — All good, what about you?
- *Más o menos.* — So-so.
- *Aquí nomás / aquí andamos.* — Just here, hanging in there (delightfully Mexican).
- *No me quejo.* — Can't complain.

One trap: **regular** in Spanish means "meh, not great" — not "regular." Answer *regular* and expect a concerned *¿por qué, qué pasó?*

## Hear it in context

**Running into a friend (casual, Mexican):**

> — ¡Ey! ¿Qué onda? ¿Cómo estás?
> — ¡Qué milagro! Todo bien, aquí nomás. ¿Y tú qué cuentas?
> — Nada nuevo, mucho trabajo. Oye, hay que echarnos un café un día de estos.

*Hey! What's up? How are you? — Long time no see! All good, just hanging in. And what's new with you? — Nothing new, lots of work. Hey, we should grab a coffee one of these days.*

**Greeting a client (formal):**

> — Buenos días, licenciada. ¿Cómo está?
> — Muy bien, gracias, ¿y usted?
> — Bien, gracias. ¿Le parece si empezamos?

*Good morning, ma'am. How are you? — Very well, thank you, and you? — Well, thanks. Shall we get started?*

## Common mistakes

- **Using "¿cómo estás?" with everyone.** With your friend's grandfather, your professor, or any *usted* relationship, drop the -s: *¿cómo está?* The one letter is the whole difference between casual and respectful.
- **"¿Cómo eres?" instead of "¿cómo estás?"** *Ser* asks about identity, *estar* about state. *¿Cómo eres?* = "what kind of person are you?" — a strange thing to ask the barista.
- **Answering "regular" to mean "fine."** In Spanish it signals things are a bit off. Say *bien* or *todo bien*.
- **Opening with "¿qué onda?" in formal settings.** It's friends-and-peers slang. A job interview starts with *buenos días, ¿cómo está?*
- **Forgetting to ask back.** *Bien, gracias* full stop sounds abrupt. The rhythm is answer + *¿y tú?* / *¿y usted?* — always return the ball.

## FAQ

**What's the difference between "¿cómo estás?" and "¿cómo está?"**
*Estás* = informal *tú* (friends, peers, kids). *Está* = formal *usted* (elders, strangers, professional settings). Same question, different level of respect.

**What does "¿qué onda?" literally mean?**
"What wave?" — Mexican slang for "what's up?". Friends only; answer with *nada, aquí nomás* or just *¿qué onda?* back.

**How do I ask "how have you been?"**
*¿Cómo has estado?* — perfect for someone you haven't seen in weeks. Even warmer: *¡Cuánto tiempo! ¿Cómo has estado?*

**Is "¿qué tal?" informal?**
It's casual but remarkably safe — you can use *¿qué tal?* in almost any situation short of the most formal ones.
