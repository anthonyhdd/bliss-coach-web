Happy birthday in Spanish is **¡feliz cumpleaños!** — and if the birthday person is Mexican, you should also know two more things: the affectionate short form *¡feliz cumple!*, and the fact that Mexicans don't sing "Happy Birthday to You" at all. They sing **Las Mañanitas**, a completely different (and much prettier) song. Get the phrase, the variations, and the traditions here, and you'll never just mumble the one line you memorized.

## The main way to say it

**¡Feliz cumpleaños!** — pronounced **feh-LEES koom-pleh-AH-nyohs**.

Breaking it down:

- *Feliz* = happy; *cumpleaños* = birthday, built from *cumplir* (to complete/turn) + *años* (years). A birthday in Spanish is literally the "completing of years" — which is why Spanish also asks *¿cuántos años cumples?* ("how many years are you turning?").
- The **ñ** is its own letter, pronounced like the *ny* in "canyon": ah-**NYOHS**. This one matters — see the mistakes section for why.
- The word **always ends in -s**, even for one birthday. *Cumpleaños* is one of those Spanish nouns (like *paraguas*) whose singular form already ends in s: *el cumpleaños*, *los cumpleaños*. There is no such word as *cumpleaño*.

In Mexico you'll constantly hear the clipped, affectionate **¡feliz cumple!** between friends — the Spanish equivalent of "happy bday!" It's warm, casual, and completely standard in speech and texts.

## Variations

| Phrase | Meaning | When to use it |
|--------|---------|----------------|
| ¡Feliz cumpleaños! | Happy birthday! | Universal, any register |
| ¡Feliz cumple! | Happy bday! | Casual — friends, texts, very common in Mexico |
| ¡Muchas felicidades! | Congratulations! / All the best! | Extremely common on birthdays in Mexico |
| ¡Felicidades en tu día! | Congratulations on your day! | Warm, card-worthy |
| ¡Que cumplas muchos más! | May you have many more! | Classic follow-up wish |
| ¡Que todos tus deseos se hagan realidad! | May all your wishes come true! | Sweet, for cards and toasts |
| ¡Feliz vuelta al sol! | Happy trip around the sun! | Trendy, social-media flavored |
| ¡Que te la pases muy bien! | Have a great one! | Very Mexican — the standard party wish |

Note that **¡felicidades!** on its own is a totally normal birthday greeting in Mexico — you don't have to say *cumpleaños* at all. A very natural combo: *¡Muchas felicidades! ¿Cómo te la estás pasando?* ("Congratulations! How's your day going?")

**The cultural package.** In Mexico, birthdays come with rituals worth knowing:

- **Las Mañanitas** — the traditional birthday song, sung at parties, at midnight, or as a dawn serenade. First line: *Estas son las mañanitas que cantaba el rey David...* Nobody expects you to nail every verse, but recognizing it (and humming along) earns you serious points.
- **La mordida** — after the song, the crowd chants *¡mor-di-da! ¡mor-di-da!* ("bite!") and the birthday person takes a bite of the cake with no hands... at which point someone traditionally pushes their face into it. All love, mostly frosting.

## Hear it in context

**Texting a friend at midnight (casual):**

> — ¡¡Feliz cumple!! 🎉 Que te la pases increíble hoy.
> — ¡Gracias! ¿Vienes a la fiesta el sábado?
> — ¡Claro que sí! ¿Qué le llevo al festejado?
> — Nada, solo ganas de bailar.

*Happy bday!! Have an amazing day. — Thanks! Are you coming to the party Saturday? — Of course! What should I bring for the birthday boy? — Nothing, just be ready to dance.*

**At the party, cake time (festive):**

> — ¡Muchas felicidades, tía! Que cumplas muchos años más.
> — Gracias, mi amor. ¡Vengan todos, ya vamos a partir el pastel!
> — ¡Primero Las Mañanitas! Y luego... ¡mordida, mordida!

*Happy birthday, auntie! May you have many more. — Thank you, sweetheart. Everyone come, we're about to cut the cake! — Las Mañanitas first! And then... bite, bite!*

## Common mistakes

- **"Feliz cumpleaño."** No final -s = not a word. *Cumpleaños* keeps its s even in the singular: *mi cumpleaños es en julio*.
- **Skipping the ñ.** *Años* means years; *anos*... means anuses. *Feliz cumpleanos* in writing (no tilde) is forgiven; saying "ah-nos" out loud is the most infamous beginner blooper in Spanish. Practice that *ny* sound.
- **"Feliz nacimiento."** *Nacimiento* is birth (the event, or a nativity scene) — nobody says this for birthdays.
- **Assuming "felicidades" is only for achievements.** On birthdays, *¡felicidades!* is not just acceptable — in Mexico it's one of the most common things to say.
- **Singing "Happy Birthday" translated.** The English melody exists in Mexico, but the real tradition is *Las Mañanitas*. At a Mexican party, wait for it.

## FAQ

**Is it "feliz cumpleaños" or "felices cumpleaños"?**
*Feliz cumpleaños.* The noun looks plural but functions as singular here, so *feliz* stays singular.

**How do you say "happy belated birthday"?**
*¡Feliz cumpleaños atrasado!* — a totally normal thing to say (or text) in Mexico when you missed the day.

**What is Las Mañanitas?**
Mexico's traditional birthday song, sung instead of "Happy Birthday to You" — at parties, at midnight, or as an early-morning serenade.

**How do I ask someone's age politely?**
*¿Cuántos años cumples?* ("how many are you turning?") is the friendly party version. With adults you don't know well... maybe just don't.
