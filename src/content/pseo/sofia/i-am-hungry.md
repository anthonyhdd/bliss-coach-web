I'm hungry in Spanish is **tengo hambre** — and the very first thing to notice is that Spanish doesn't say you *are* hungry; it says you *have* hunger. *Tengo* = "I have," *hambre* = "hunger." This "have + noun" pattern runs through a whole family of body states (thirst, cold, sleepiness, fear), so nailing it here unlocks a dozen more phrases. In Mexico you'll also hear playful, exaggerated ways to say you're starving.

## The main way to say it

**Tengo hambre** — pronounced **TEN-goh AHM-breh**.

The pronunciation details that matter:

- **Tengo** stresses the first syllable: **TEN**-goh, with a hard "g."
- **Hambre** starts with a silent *h*: it's "AHM-breh," never "HAM." Stress on the first syllable.
- Run together: *TEN-goh-AHM-breh*.

The grammar that matters: this is the **tener + noun** construction. You *have* the state:

- **Tengo hambre** = I'm hungry (I have hunger)
- **Tengo sed** = I'm thirsty (I have thirst)
- **Tengo sueño** = I'm sleepy
- **Tengo frío / calor** = I'm cold / hot

To intensify, add **mucha** (*hambre* is feminine): **tengo mucha hambre** ("I'm very hungry"). Don't use *muy* here — that's a classic trap covered below.

## Variations

| Phrase | Meaning | Register |
|--------|---------|----------|
| Tengo hambre | I'm hungry | Universal |
| Tengo mucha hambre | I'm very hungry | Universal |
| Me estoy muriendo de hambre | I'm starving (dying of hunger) | Casual, emphatic |
| Traigo un hambre… | I'm so hungry (Mexican phrasing) | Casual, Mexican |
| Me ruge el estómago | My stomach is growling | Casual, vivid |
| Ando muerto / muerta de hambre | I'm starving | Casual, Mexican |
| ¿Ya vamos a comer? | Are we going to eat yet? | Casual hint |
| Me dio hambre | I got hungry | Casual |
| Tengo un poco de hambre | I'm a little hungry | Softer |

For real drama, Mexicans love **me estoy muriendo de hambre** ("I'm dying of hunger" = starving) and **traigo un hambre que no veo** ("I'm so hungry I can't see straight"). The verb *traer* ("to carry/bring") standing in for *tener* — *traigo hambre* — is a very Mexican coloring.

To be polite and hint rather than announce, **¿ya vamos a comer?** ("shall we eat now?") does the job without a blunt declaration.

## Hear it in context

**Deciding to eat with a friend (casual):**

> — Oye, ¿ya vamos a comer? Traigo un hambre…
> — Yo también, me estoy muriendo de hambre.
> — ¿Tacos?
> — Órale, vamos. Yo ya no aguanto.

*Hey, shall we eat now? I'm so hungry… — Me too, I'm starving. — Tacos? — Cool, let's go. I can't hold out anymore.*

**Kid telling a parent (everyday):**

> — Mamá, tengo hambre.
> — Ya casi está la comida, aguántate tantito.
> — Es que tengo mucha hambre, me ruge el estómago.
> — Ándale, ten una fruta mientras.

*Mom, I'm hungry. — Food's almost ready, hold on a little. — But I'm really hungry, my stomach's growling. — Here, have a fruit in the meantime.*

## Common mistakes

- **"Estoy hambre" or "soy hambre."** The single most common error. You don't *be* hungry in Spanish; you *have* hunger: *tengo hambre*. Never *estoy/soy hambre*.
- **"Tengo muy hambre."** Wrong intensifier. *Hambre* is a noun, so it takes *mucha* (a quantity word), not *muy* (which modifies adjectives): *tengo **mucha** hambre*.
- **Pronouncing the "h" in "hambre."** It's silent: "AHM-breh." No breathy "H."
- **Saying "el hambre" and then treating it as masculine.** It's spelled *el hambre* (to avoid two *a* sounds clashing) but it's a **feminine** noun — hence *much**a** hambre*, not *mucho*.

## Mexico vs Spain notes

*Tengo hambre* / *tengo mucha hambre* are completely standard everywhere.

- **Traer** in place of *tener* (*traigo hambre*) is a Mexican/Latin American colloquial flavor; a Spaniard would say *tengo hambre* or *tengo un hambre que me muero*.
- Spain also loves emphatic starvation phrases — **estoy muerto de hambre** — which Mexico uses too (*ando muerto de hambre*).
- Pronunciation is regionally neutral here; nothing lisps.

## FAQ

**Why is it "tengo hambre" and not "estoy hambre"?**
Spanish expresses hunger as something you *have*, not something you *are*: *tengo hambre* = "I have hunger." The same pattern covers *tengo sed* (thirsty), *tengo sueño* (sleepy), and *tengo frío* (cold).

**How do I say "very hungry"?**
*Tengo mucha hambre.* Because *hambre* is a noun, you intensify it with *mucha* (a quantity), not *muy*. *Tengo muy hambre* is incorrect.

**Is "hambre" masculine or feminine?**
Feminine. It's written *el hambre* (to avoid the clash of two *a* sounds), but it behaves as feminine — so *mucha hambre*, *el hambre desesperada*.

**How do I say "I'm starving"?**
*Me estoy muriendo de hambre* ("I'm dying of hunger") or *estoy muerto/muerta de hambre*. In Mexico, *traigo un hambre…* is a very natural casual version.

**Is the "h" in "hambre" pronounced?**
No — the *h* is silent, as in all Spanish words. It's "AHM-breh."
