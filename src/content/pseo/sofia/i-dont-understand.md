To say "I don't understand" in Spanish, say **no entiendo** — and congratulations, you've just learned the most honest sentence in language learning. Every learner says it hundreds of times, and that's exactly how it should be: it keeps conversations alive instead of ending them. The real skill is what you say *next* — asking people to repeat, slow down, or rephrase — and Mexican Spanish has a beautiful little word for that: **¿Mande?**

## The main way to say it

**No entiendo** — pronounced *noh en-TYEN-doh*.

From the verb *entender* (to understand). Present tense, first person, job done:

- **Perdón, no entiendo.** — Sorry, I don't understand.
- **No entiendo, ¿me lo puedes explicar?** — I don't understand, can you explain it to me?

There's a useful sibling: **no entendí** (*noh en-ten-DEE*) — "I didn't understand / I didn't catch that." Use *no entiendo* for something you still don't get (a concept, a word), and *no entendí* for the sentence that just flew past your ears. Mexicans use the past-tense version constantly for exactly that just-missed-it moment.

## Variations

| Spanish | Pronunciation | Register | When to use it |
|---|---|---|---|
| No entiendo | noh en-TYEN-doh | Universal | "I don't understand" — the default |
| No entendí | noh en-ten-DEE | Universal | "I didn't catch that" — for the last sentence |
| No le entiendo | noh leh en-TYEN-doh | Formal | Same, addressing someone as *usted* |
| ¿Mande? | MAHN-deh | Very Mexican | "Pardon? What was that?" — Mexico's signature |
| ¿Cómo? | KOH-moh | Casual | "What? Come again?" |
| ¿Puede repetir, por favor? | PWEH-deh reh-peh-TEER | Polite | "Can you repeat, please?" |
| Más despacio, por favor | mahs dehs-PAH-syoh | Universal | "Slower, please" — the learner's lifeline |
| No capté / No caché | noh kahp-TEH / kah-CHEH | Slang | "I didn't get it" — casual Mexican |
| No agarré la onda | noh ah-gah-RREH lah OHN-dah | Slang | "I didn't catch the vibe/point" — very Mexican |

**¿Mande?** deserves its own paragraph. Where Spain says *¿Qué?* or *¿Cómo?*, Mexico says *¿Mande?* — literally "command me," a leftover of old courtesy that became the universal Mexican "pardon?" It's polite, it's instant, and using it is one of the fastest ways to sound like you learned your Spanish in Mexico. Bare **¿Qué?** in response to a person, by contrast, can sound brusque to Mexican ears — many Mexican kids are literally corrected by their parents: *"¡Se dice mande!"*

And **no agarré la onda** — literally "I didn't grab the wave" — runs on *onda*, the great Mexican all-purpose vibe-word (*¿qué onda?* = what's up, *buena onda* = cool/nice). It means you missed the point or the joke, not the words.

## Hear it in context

**Dialog 1 — Asking someone to slow down**

> — Entonces agarras el metro hasta Balderas, haces transbordo y te bajas en...
> — **Perdón, no entendí. ¿Puedes hablar más despacio, por favor? Apenas estoy aprendiendo español.**
> — ¡Claro! Metro. Hasta. Balderas. ¿Va?
>
> — So you take the metro to Balderas, transfer, and get off at...
> — Sorry, I didn't catch that. Can you speak slower, please? I'm just learning Spanish.
> — Of course! Metro. To. Balderas. Okay?

**Dialog 2 — The ¿Mande? reflex**

> — Oye, ¿me pasas el control?
> — **¿Mande?**
> — Que si me pasas el control de la tele.
> — ¡Ah! Sí, aquí está.
>
> — Hey, can you pass me the remote?
> — Pardon?
> — I said, can you pass me the TV remote.
> — Oh! Yes, here it is.

Notice the repair pattern in dialog 2: the repeat starts with **que** — *"Que si me pasas..."* ("I said/I was asking if..."). Recognizing that little *que* tells you a repetition is coming.

## Common mistakes

1. **Saying "no entender."** That's the infinitive — "to not understand." Conjugate it: *no entiendo*. Tarzan-Spanish is understood but easily avoided.

2. **Responding "¿Qué?" to people.** Fine for things (*¿Qué es eso?*), abrupt for people in Mexico. Train the *¿Mande?* reflex instead — or at minimum *¿Cómo?*

3. **Confusing "no entiendo" with "no sé."** *No sé* = I don't know (information). *No entiendo* = I don't understand (meaning). Answering *no sé* when someone asks if you understood them is genuinely confusing.

4. **Faking comprehension.** Nodding through *no entiendo* moments is the number-one conversation killer for learners. Mexicans are famously patient with learners — saying *no entendí, ¿me lo repites?* earns you a slower, friendlier rerun almost every time.

5. **Saying "no comprendo" everywhere.** It's correct, but bookish — in everyday Mexican speech, *entender* wins over *comprender* by a mile. *No comprendo* sounds a bit like a phrasebook talking.

## FAQ

**What's the difference between "no entiendo" and "no entendí"?**
Tense and target. *No entiendo* (present): I don't understand this — still. *No entendí* (past): I didn't catch what you just said. For a missed sentence, *no entendí* is the more precise, more native-sounding choice.

**Is "¿Mande?" only used in Mexico?**
Essentially, yes — it's a hallmark of Mexican Spanish. Elsewhere people say *¿Cómo?*, *¿Perdón?*, or *¿Diga?* You'll be understood everywhere, but *¿Mande?* marks you as Mexico-trained.

**How do I say "Do you speak English?"**
**¿Habla inglés?** (formal) / **¿Hablas inglés?** (informal). Pair it with *estoy aprendiendo español* — "I'm learning Spanish" — and watch people switch into patient-teacher mode.

**How do I ask what a specific word means?**
**¿Qué significa...?** — *¿Qué significa "chamba"?* ("What does 'chamba' mean?" Answer: it's Mexican slang for work/job.)
