How much is it in Spanish is **¿cuánto cuesta?** — the phrase that unlocks every market stall, taco cart, and corner shop in the Spanish-speaking world. But natives split the question two ways: **¿cuánto cuesta?** ("how much does it cost?") for a single item, and **¿cuánto es?** ("how much is it [all together]?") when you're paying at the register. Get both, and you'll never be stuck pointing and guessing.

## The main way to say it

**¿Cuánto cuesta?** — pronounced **KWAN-toh KWES-tah**.

The pronunciation details that matter:

- **Cuánto** is two syllables: **KWAN**-toh, stress on the first. The *cu-* is a hard "kw" sound, like the start of English "quantity."
- **Cuesta** is **KWES**-tah — again a "kw" opening, stress on the first syllable.
- The accent mark on *cuánto* is there because it's a question word; it doesn't change the sound much, but it flags this as *asking* how much, not stating it.

The two everyday forms:

- **¿Cuánto cuesta?** = how much does *it* cost — one item. *¿Cuánto cuesta esta playera?* ("how much is this T-shirt?")
- **¿Cuánto cuestan?** = how much do *they* cost — plural items. *¿Cuánto cuestan los mangos?* ("how much are the mangos?")
- **¿Cuánto es?** = how much is it — the total, usually at checkout. Fast, natural, exactly what you say when handing over your basket.

A softer, very common variant is **¿qué precio tiene?** ("what price does it have?"), which sounds a touch more formal and polite.

## Variations

| Phrase | Meaning | Register |
|--------|---------|----------|
| ¿Cuánto cuesta? | How much does it cost? | Universal |
| ¿Cuánto cuestan? | How much do they cost? (plural) | Universal |
| ¿Cuánto es? | How much is it (total)? | Everyday, at the register |
| ¿Cuánto le debo? | How much do I owe you? | Polite, warm — very Mexican |
| ¿Qué precio tiene? | What's the price? | Slightly formal |
| ¿A cómo? / ¿A cómo el kilo? | How much a kilo? (markets) | Casual, Mexican markets |
| ¿Cuánto vale? | How much is it worth? | Common, universal |
| ¿Me da precio? | Can you give me a price? | Casual, haggling |

At a Mexican *mercado*, the phrase you'll actually hear thrown at vendors is **¿a cómo?** — short for *¿a cómo está?* ("what's it going for?"). *¿A cómo el kilo de jitomate?* ("how much a kilo of tomatoes?") sounds like a local, not a tourist reading from a phrasebook.

To ask the total you owe warmly, **¿cuánto le debo?** ("how much do I owe you?") is the courteous Mexican move — it treats the exchange as personal rather than transactional.

## Hear it in context

**Buying at a market stall (casual):**

> — ¿A cómo los aguacates?
> — A treinta el kilo, güerito.
> — Deme medio kilo, por favor. ¿Cuánto le debo?
> — Quince pesos.

*How much for the avocados? — Thirty a kilo, boss. — Give me half a kilo, please. How much do I owe you? — Fifteen pesos.*

**Paying at a café (everyday):**

> — Un café y una concha, por favor.
> — Claro. ¿Algo más?
> — No, así está bien. ¿Cuánto es?
> — Son cuarenta y cinco pesos.

*A coffee and a pastry, please. — Sure. Anything else? — No, that's all. How much is it? — That's forty-five pesos.*

## Common mistakes

- **Mixing up "cuesta" and "cuestan."** One item takes *cuesta*, plural items take *cuestan*: *¿cuánto cuesta**n** los zapatos?* Getting the verb to agree marks you as careful.
- **Saying "cómo mucho es."** That's a word-for-word calque of "how much." The word for "how much" is *cuánto*, not *cómo mucho*.
- **Using "¿cuánto cuesta?" at checkout.** When you're paying for everything at once, natives say *¿cuánto es?* — the total. *¿Cuánto cuesta?* there sounds like you're still asking about one item.
- **Forgetting the accent in writing.** In text it's *cuánto* (with the accent). Without it, *cuanto* means "as much as" — a completely different word.

## Mexico vs Spain notes

The core question is identical across the Spanish-speaking world — *¿cuánto cuesta?* and *¿cuánto es?* work everywhere. The differences are flavor:

- **¿A cómo?** is very Latin American and especially Mexican in markets. In Spain you're more likely to hear *¿a cuánto está?*
- **Currency words change**: in Mexico it's *pesos*, in Spain *euros*. The question doesn't change, just the answer.
- **¿Cuánto vale?** is used everywhere but is a touch more common in Spain for everyday shopping, where Mexicans lean on *¿cuánto cuesta?* and *¿cuánto es?*

## FAQ

**Is it "cuánto cuesta" or "cuánto es"?**
Both are correct but for different moments. *¿Cuánto cuesta?* asks the price of a specific item; *¿cuánto es?* asks the total when you're paying. Use *cuesta* while browsing, *es* at the register.

**How do I ask the price of more than one thing?**
Switch to the plural verb: *¿cuánto cuesta**n**?* — *¿cuánto cuestan las naranjas?* ("how much are the oranges?").

**What does "¿a cómo?" mean?**
It's casual Mexican market shorthand for "what's the price / what's it going for," short for *¿a cómo está?*. *¿A cómo el kilo?* means "how much per kilo?"

**How do I say "how much do I owe you?"**
*¿Cuánto le debo?* — polite and warm, using *usted*. It's the friendly Mexican way to ask for your total.

**Do I need the accent on "cuánto"?**
In writing, yes — *cuánto* with the accent is the question word. *Cuanto* without it means "as much as." In speech the difference is context, not sound.
