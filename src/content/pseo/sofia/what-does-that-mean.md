What does that mean in Spanish is **¿qué significa?** — the single most useful phrase for anyone actually learning the language, because it turns every confusing word into a lesson. Right next to it is **¿qué quiere decir?** ("what does it mean," literally "what does it want to say"), which sounds a touch more conversational. Master both and you can keep any conversation going instead of nodding and hoping.

## The main way to say it

**¿Qué significa?** — pronounced **keh sig-nee-FEE-kah**.

The pronunciation details that matter:

- **Qué** is one syllable: **keh**, with a short "eh." The *qu-* is a hard "k"; the *u* is silent, as always in Spanish *que/qui*.
- **Significa** has the stress on the **FEE**: sig-nee-**FEE**-kah. Four syllables, evenly clipped.
- The accent on **qué** marks it as the question word "what." It carries a small extra push in the voice.

To ask about a *specific* word or phrase, add **eso** ("that") or the word itself:

- **¿Qué significa eso?** = what does *that* mean? (pointing at something just said)
- **¿Qué significa "órale"?** = what does "órale" mean?
- **¿Qué quiere decir esa palabra?** = what does that word mean?

**¿Qué quiere decir?** is the everyday twin of *¿qué significa?*. They're interchangeable in almost every situation; *significa* leans slightly more "dictionary definition," *quiere decir* slightly more "what are you getting at."

## Variations

| Phrase | Meaning | Register |
|--------|---------|----------|
| ¿Qué significa? | What does it mean? | Universal |
| ¿Qué significa eso? | What does that mean? | Universal |
| ¿Qué quiere decir? | What does it mean? | Everyday, conversational |
| ¿Qué quiere decir eso? | What does that mean? | Everyday |
| ¿Qué es eso? | What is that? | Casual |
| ¿A qué te refieres? | What do you mean? (what are you referring to) | Conversational |
| ¿Cómo? | What? / Come again? | Casual, asking to repeat |
| ¿Qué quisiste decir? | What did you mean (by that)? | Conversational, past |

There's an important split hiding here. **¿Qué significa?** asks for the *definition* of a word. **¿A qué te refieres?** asks what a *person* means by what they just said — you understood the words but not the intent. If a friend says something cryptic, *¿a qué te refieres?* ("what do you mean by that?") is the phrase, not *¿qué significa?*.

## Hear it in context

**Asking about an unfamiliar word (learner-friendly):**

> — Ese lugar está bien chido.
> — Perdón, ¿qué significa "chido"?
> — Ah, quiere decir "cool," "padre." Que está muy bueno.
> — Ah, ¡ya entendí! Gracias.

*That place is really "chido." — Sorry, what does "chido" mean? — Ah, it means "cool," "great." That it's really good. — Oh, I get it now! Thanks.*

**Clarifying someone's intent (conversational):**

> — Creo que mejor lo dejamos para después.
> — ¿A qué te refieres? ¿Cancelamos?
> — No, no. Solo lo movemos al viernes.
> — Ah, ok, eso sí.

*I think we'd better leave it for later. — What do you mean? Are we cancelling? — No, no. We just move it to Friday. — Oh, okay, that works.*

## Common mistakes

- **Saying "qué significa esto/eso" when you mean a person's intent.** For "what do you mean (by that)?" use *¿a qué te refieres?* or *¿qué quieres decir?*. *¿Qué significa?* asks about a word's definition.
- **Pronouncing the "u" in "qué."** It's silent: *qué* = "keh," not "kweh." The *u* only exists to keep the *q* hard.
- **Word-for-word "qué hace eso significa."** Spanish doesn't use a "does" helper. It's simply *¿qué significa eso?* — three words.
- **Forgetting the accent on "qué."** In writing, the question word takes an accent: *¿qué significa?*. Without it, *que* is a linking word ("that").

## Mexico vs Spain notes

Both *¿qué significa?* and *¿qué quiere decir?* are fully standard across the Spanish-speaking world — no regional swap needed.

- The pronunciation is essentially identical in Mexico and Spain; there's no *c/z* in *significa*, so the Castilian lisp never appears.
- In casual Spain you'll hear a quick **¿qué?** or **¿cómo?** to ask for a repeat, exactly as in Mexico. Mexicans also use **¿mande?** as a polite "come again?" — a very local courtesy you won't hear in Spain.
- Slang words differ by country, so *¿qué significa?* is doubly useful when traveling: it's your key to decoding *chido* in Mexico, *guay* in Spain, or *bacán* elsewhere.

## FAQ

**Is it "qué significa" or "qué quiere decir"?**
Both mean "what does it mean" and are interchangeable in most cases. *Qué significa* leans toward a dictionary definition; *qué quiere decir* is slightly more conversational. Either is safe.

**How do I ask what a specific word means?**
Put the word in quotes or after the phrase: *¿qué significa "órale"?* or *¿qué quiere decir esa palabra?* ("what does that word mean?").

**What's the difference between "¿qué significa?" and "¿a qué te refieres?"**
*¿Qué significa?* asks for the meaning of a word. *¿A qué te refieres?* asks what a *person* means by what they said — you understood the words but not the point.

**Why is the "u" in "qué" silent?**
In Spanish, *qu* before *e* or *i* is just a hard "k" sound; the *u* is a spelling convention and never pronounced. So *qué* is "keh."

**What's "¿mande?" in Mexico?**
It's a polite Mexican way to say "sorry, what?" or "come again?" when you didn't catch something. It's very common and courteous, and specific to Mexican Spanish.
