Take care in Spanish is **cuídate** — the warm, caring goodbye you give a friend, and one of the most useful sign-offs to have ready. Because Spanish marks formality, "take care" splits into **cuídate** (informal *tú*) and **cuídese** (formal *usted*), and Mexicans layer on affectionate extras like *cuídate mucho* and *que te vaya bien*. This little verb comes from *cuidar* ("to take care of"), and getting its endings right is a quick win.

## The main way to say it

**Cuídate** — pronounced **KWEE-dah-teh**.

The pronunciation details that matter:

- The stress is on the first syllable — that's what the accent on **cuí** marks: **KWEE**-dah-teh.
- **Cui-** is a "kwee" sound (hard "k" + "wee"), like the start of "queen."
- The final **-te** is a soft "teh." Three clean syllables.

The formality split:

- **Cuídate** = take care (informal, to a friend, family, peer). The *-te* is the reflexive *tú* ending.
- **Cuídese** = take care (formal, to an elder, stranger, or in professional settings). The *-se* is the *usted* ending.

To wish it on *plural* people (a group), it's **cuídense** ("take care, you all"). And to intensify, add **mucho**: *cuídate mucho* ("take good care of yourself"), which sounds genuinely affectionate.

## Variations

| Phrase | Meaning | Register |
|--------|---------|----------|
| Cuídate | Take care (informal) | Casual, warm |
| Cuídese | Take care (formal) | Polite, formal |
| Cuídense | Take care (plural / group) | Casual or formal group |
| Cuídate mucho | Take good care of yourself | Warm, affectionate |
| Que te vaya bien | Hope it goes well for you | Warm, common |
| Que estés bien | Be well / stay well | Warm |
| Que te cuides | (You) take care | Casual, softened |
| Nos vemos, cuídate | See you, take care | Everyday sign-off |
| Pórtate bien | Behave / be good (playful) | Casual, joking |

The most natural everyday combo is stacking a farewell with *cuídate*: **nos vemos, cuídate** ("see you, take care"). To wish someone well on their way, **que te vaya bien** ("hope things go well for you") is warm and extremely common — practically a second "take care."

For extra affection to family or a partner, **cuídate mucho** carries real tenderness. And **pórtate bien** ("behave yourself") is the playful, joking send-off between friends.

## Hear it in context

**Saying goodbye to a friend (casual):**

> — Bueno, ya me voy, se hace tarde.
> — Va, gracias por venir. Cuídate mucho.
> — Igualmente. Nos vemos el jueves.
> — Que te vaya bien. ¡Beso!

*Alright, I'm off, it's getting late. — Cool, thanks for coming. Take good care. — You too. See you Thursday. — Hope it goes well. Bye!*

**Formal goodbye to someone older (polite):**

> — Muchas gracias por todo, doctor.
> — De nada. Cuídese, y cualquier cosa me llama.
> — Claro que sí. Que esté bien.
> — Igualmente. Hasta pronto.

*Thank you so much for everything, doctor. — You're welcome. Take care, and call me if anything comes up. — Of course. Be well. — Likewise. See you soon.*

## Common mistakes

- **Using "cuídate" with someone you should address formally.** For an elder, a stranger, or a professional context, it's *cuídese*. Mixing up *tú* and *usted* endings is the most common slip.
- **Forgetting the accent (in writing) and misplacing the stress.** It's **cuí**date — stress on the first syllable. Saying "kwee-DAH-teh" sounds off.
- **Saying "toma cuidado."** That's a Portuguese/word-for-word calque. Spanish uses the reflexive verb: *cuídate*, not *toma cuidado*.
- **Using the singular for a group.** To a group it's *cuídense* (plural), not *cuídate*.

## Mexico vs Spain notes

*Cuídate* / *cuídese* work identically across the Spanish-speaking world.

- **Que te vaya bien** is universal and beloved in both Mexico and Spain as a warm send-off.
- In **Spain**, addressing a group informally uses *vosotros*, so "take care, you all" becomes **cuidaos** — a form Mexicans never use (Mexico uses *cuídense* for every plural). This is one of the clearest Mexico–Spain grammar differences.
- Pronunciation is regionally neutral; nothing lisps in *cuídate*.

## FAQ

**Is it "cuídate" or "cuídese"?**
*Cuídate* is informal (to a friend, family, or peer — the *tú* form); *cuídese* is formal (to an elder, stranger, or in professional settings — the *usted* form). Match it to how you'd otherwise address the person.

**How do I say "take care" to a group?**
Use *cuídense* ("take care, you all"). In Spain, an informal group would instead hear *cuidaos*, but Mexico uses *cuídense* for every plural.

**Why is there an accent on "cuídate"?**
It marks the stress on the first syllable: **cuí**-da-te (KWEE-dah-teh). Without knowing it, learners tend to stress the wrong syllable.

**What's the difference between "cuídate" and "que te vaya bien"?**
*Cuídate* literally tells someone to take care of themselves; *que te vaya bien* wishes that things go well for them. They're often said together as a warm goodbye.

**Can I make it more affectionate?**
Yes — add *mucho*: *cuídate mucho* ("take good care of yourself"). It's tender and common with family, close friends, and partners.
