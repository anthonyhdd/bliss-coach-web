The KPMG interview process typically moves from an online application and assessments through behavioral interviews to a final partner or assessment-center round. Across audit, tax, advisory, and technology, candidates commonly report a strong emphasis on the firm's values, teamwork, and client service, with case or technical elements layered on depending on the practice.

## The KPMG interview process

KPMG doesn't publish one universal pipeline, and steps vary by country, function, and level. Candidates commonly report a sequence along these lines:

1. **Online application and resume screen.** Recruiters filter for academics, relevant experience, and genuine interest in the function.
2. **Online assessments.** Many early-career processes report situational-judgment, game-based, or reasoning assessments.
3. **Video or recruiter interview.** A recorded or live conversation covering motivation ("Why KPMG? Why this function?") and initial behavioral questions.
4. **Case or technical round (role-dependent).** Advisory candidates commonly report case interviews; audit and tax roles report technical questions on accounting, audit, or tax; technology roles report role-specific technical questions.
5. **Final interview / assessment center.** Often a partner or director interview, sometimes as part of an assessment day with group exercises, a presentation, and individual interviews.

Timelines vary; campus recruiting follows a seasonal calendar, and experienced hires commonly report a few weeks across rounds.

## Questions KPMG commonly asks

These reflect what candidates frequently report, with what each is probing:

1. **"Why KPMG, and why this function?"** — Tests whether you understand the firm's businesses and can be specific rather than generic.
2. **"Tell me about a time you worked as part of a team."** — Client work is collaborative; they want evidence you contribute and share credit.
3. **"Describe a time you managed competing deadlines."** — Probes the time management busy season demands.
4. **"Tell me about a time you delivered great service to a client or customer."** — Maps to KPMG's client focus.
5. **"Give an example of when you showed leadership or initiative."** — Looks for drive, not just a title.
6. **"Tell me about a time you made a mistake and how you handled it."** — Checks integrity and ownership, central to audit and tax.
7. **"Describe a complex problem you solved."** — Probes structured thinking; in advisory this may become a case.
8. **"Tell me about a time you had to learn something quickly."** — Ties to the firm's growth-mindset framing.
9. **"Walk me through your resume."** — Sets the tone; they want a clear narrative.

## What KPMG looks for

KPMG publicly frames its purpose around inspiring confidence and empowering change, and its stated values emphasize integrity, excellence, courage, working together, and doing what matters ("for better"). In interviews, that translates into evidence that you act with integrity, collaborate genuinely, take initiative, and put clients first. The firm publicly emphasizes diverse backgrounds and continuous learning, so demonstrating curiosity and adaptability lands well.

Practically, interviewers are listening for teamwork, client focus, integrity, resilience under deadline pressure, and — for advisory — structured problem-solving.

### A worked STAR answer

**Question: "Tell me about a time you showed integrity."**

- **Situation:** While reviewing figures for a group deliverable, I noticed a number that didn't reconcile with the source data.
- **Task:** The deadline was close, and it would have been easy to let a small discrepancy pass.
- **Action:** I flagged it, traced it back to a mislabeled input, and corrected it even though it meant reworking part of the analysis and admitting the error to the group.
- **Result:** The final numbers held up, and the team trusted the deliverable because it was right rather than just finished. I learned that in work built on trust, catching your own errors is non-negotiable.

## FAQ

**How long does the KPMG interview process take?**
It varies by function and region. Campus processes follow a seasonal calendar; experienced hires commonly report a few weeks across assessments and interviews.

**Does KPMG use online assessments?**
Many early-career processes commonly report situational-judgment, game-based, or reasoning assessments. Prepare for the specific format your application uses.

**Are KPMG interviews case-based?**
Advisory roles commonly report case interviews. Audit, tax, and technology roles lean more toward behavioral fit plus role-specific technical questions.

**What values does KPMG interview for?**
Publicly, KPMG emphasizes integrity, excellence, courage, working together, and doing what matters. Prepare stories that demonstrate these clearly.
