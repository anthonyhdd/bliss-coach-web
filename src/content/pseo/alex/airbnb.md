Airbnb's interview process pairs standard functional rounds with something distinctive: dedicated **core values interviews**, commonly conducted by employees outside the hiring team, that weigh as heavily as technical performance. If you prepare only for the skills portion and improvise the values portion, you're preparing for half the loop.

## The Airbnb interview process

Airbnb doesn't publish a fixed loop and details vary by role, but candidates commonly report this structure:

1. **Recruiter screen.** A 20–30 minute call on background, motivation, and logistics. Expect an early, genuine "why Airbnb?" — the company's mission of belonging is central to its identity, and recruiters listen for real connection to the product.
2. **Functional screen(s).** Engineers report one or two coding interviews, often emphasizing practical, product-relevant problems over pure puzzles; product, design, and business candidates report portfolio, case, or experience-based screens.
3. **Virtual onsite.** Typically four to six interviews. For engineers: coding, system or product architecture, and a past-experience deep dive. For other functions: role-specific rounds plus cross-functional conversations.
4. **Core values interviews.** Usually one or two sessions, commonly run by trained employees from *outside* the team you'd join. These are conversational but deliberate: they explore your motivations, how you treat people, and how you relate to Airbnb's mission. Candidates consistently report that a "no" here can sink an otherwise strong loop.
5. **Decision and offer.** Feedback from all interviewers is consolidated; commonly reported timelines run three to five weeks.

The practical takeaway: Airbnb treats values fit as a first-class hiring signal, publicly and by design — founder Brian Chesky has spoken openly about protecting culture through hiring. Prepare for those rounds with the same rigor as the technical ones.

## Behavioral questions Airbnb asks

Commonly reported questions and what each probes:

1. **"Why Airbnb?"** — The single most important question in the loop. Tests genuine connection to the product and mission; candidates who share a real host or travel story consistently report it landing well.
2. **"What does belonging mean to you?"** — Probes alignment with Airbnb's stated mission of creating a world where anyone can belong anywhere — in your own words, not theirs.
3. **"Tell me about a time you went out of your way to make someone feel welcome or supported."** — Maps to the company's published "Be a Host" value; tests hospitality as a behavior, not a slogan.
4. **"Describe a risk you took that others thought was crazy. What happened?"** — Reflects Airbnb's publicly celebrated scrappy origin story; probes bold, creative problem-solving.
5. **"Tell me about a time you were resourceful with very little."** — Tests entrepreneurial creativity — doing more with less, a theme in the company's public lore.
6. **"Describe a time you championed something you believed in against resistance."** — Maps to the published "Champion the Mission" value; probes conviction and follow-through.
7. **"Tell me about a time you received hard feedback. How did you respond?"** — Tests openness and low ego — important where values interviewers evaluate how you'll treat colleagues.
8. **"Describe a conflict with a teammate and how it was resolved."** — Probes empathy and directness in tension; hosts-and-guests thinking applies internally too.
9. **"Tell me about a time you embraced a big change you didn't choose."** — Maps to the published "Embrace the Adventure" value; tests optimism and adaptability under uncertainty.
10. **"What's something you've created — inside or outside work — that you're proud of?"** — Probes maker energy and authentic passion; side projects, community work, and creative pursuits all count.

## What Airbnb looks for

Airbnb publishes its core values openly, and its values interviews are built around them:

- **Champion the Mission.** Commitment to "belong anywhere" — connecting your work, and your reasons for joining, to the mission of belonging.
- **Be a Host.** Care, generosity, and setting others up to succeed — with guests, teammates, and candidates alike.
- **Embrace the Adventure.** Curiosity, optimism, and comfort with the unknown; growth through change rather than resistance to it.
- **Be a Cereal Entrepreneur.** A nod to the founders' famous collectible-cereal-boxes story: bold, scrappy, creative resourcefulness.

Beyond the values, Airbnb's public product principles emphasize design quality and end-to-end user experience — so across functions, candidates who talk fluently about the *experience* their work created (not just the output) tend to align well.

## How to prepare

- **Write your "why Airbnb" story first.** If you've hosted, stayed, or had a meaningful travel experience, build your answer around it specifically. If you haven't used the product, use it before the interview — candidates report that firsthand experience noticeably strengthens the values rounds.
- **Prepare one story per core value.** Champion the Mission, Be a Host, Embrace the Adventure, Be a Cereal Entrepreneur — one real STAR story each. Our guide to [STAR method examples](/alex/blog/star-method-examples/) shows how to keep them concrete and follow-up-proof.
- **Don't parrot the values back.** Values interviewers are trained and calibrated; recited slogans read as inauthentic. Tell true stories and let the alignment show through the details.
- **Prepare functional depth for your track.** Engineers: practical coding plus architecture, with an emphasis on product thinking and code quality. Designers and PMs: expect deep interest in craft, user empathy, and end-to-end experience decisions.
- **Rehearse the whole loop, including values rounds.** Most candidates only mock the technical parts. Run at least one full values-style conversation out loud, and follow the structured plan in our guide on [how to prepare for an interview](/alex/blog/how-to-prepare-for-an-interview/) for the final week.
- **Prepare questions about culture in practice.** Asking how the team lives "Be a Host" internally, or how values show up in performance reviews, signals you take the culture as seriously as they do.

## FAQ

**What is the Airbnb core values interview?**
One or two conversational rounds, commonly conducted by trained employees outside the hiring team, exploring your motivations, how you treat people, and your connection to Airbnb's mission and published values. It's weighted seriously — strong technical performance doesn't override a values "no."

**Do I need to have used Airbnb to interview there?**
It's not a formal requirement, but candidates consistently report that authentic product experience makes the "why Airbnb" and values conversations dramatically easier. Use the product before you interview if you can.

**How long does the Airbnb interview process take?**
Commonly reported: three to five weeks from recruiter screen to decision, since feedback from functional and values interviewers is consolidated before an offer.
