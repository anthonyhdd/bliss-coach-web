The EY (Ernst & Young) interview process typically moves from an online application and assessments through behavioral and case-based interviews to a final round with senior staff. Across assurance, tax, consulting, and technology, candidates commonly report a strong emphasis on fit, teamwork, and client-service thinking, with case or technical elements layered on depending on the service line.

## The EY interview process

EY doesn't publish one universal pipeline, and the steps vary by country, service line, and level. Candidates commonly report a sequence along these lines:

1. **Online application and resume screen.** Recruiters filter for academics, relevant experience, and interest in the specific service line.
2. **Online assessments.** Many early-career processes report gamified or situational-judgment assessments and sometimes numerical or verbal reasoning tests.
3. **Video or recruiter interview.** A recorded one-way or live conversation covering motivation ("Why EY? Why this service line?") and initial behavioral questions.
4. **Case or technical round (role-dependent).** Consulting and strategy candidates commonly report case interviews; assurance and tax roles report technical questions on accounting, audit, or tax concepts; technology roles report role-specific technical questions.
5. **Final interview / assessment day.** Often a partner or senior-manager interview, sometimes as part of an assessment center with group exercises, a presentation, and individual interviews.

Timelines vary; campus recruiting follows a seasonal calendar, and experienced hires commonly report a few weeks across rounds.

## Questions EY commonly asks

These reflect what candidates frequently report, with what each is probing:

1. **"Why EY, and why this service line?"** — Tests whether you understand EY's businesses and can make a specific case beyond "Big Four prestige."
2. **"Tell me about a time you worked in a team to deliver something."** — Client work is team-based; they want evidence you contribute and share credit.
3. **"Describe a time you managed multiple deadlines or priorities."** — Probes the time management the job demands during busy season.
4. **"Tell me about a time you built a relationship with a difficult client or stakeholder."** — Maps to EY's client-service focus.
5. **"Describe a situation where you showed leadership."** — Looks for initiative, not just a title.
6. **"Tell me about a time you failed or made a mistake."** — Checks ownership and integrity, which matter especially in assurance and tax.
7. **"Give an example of when you solved a complex problem."** — Probes structured thinking; in consulting this may become a full case.
8. **"How do you keep learning and stay current?"** — Ties to EY's growth-mindset framing.
9. **"Walk me through your resume."** — Sets the tone; they want a clear narrative.

## What EY looks for

EY's public purpose is framed as "building a better working world," and its people-focused materials emphasize integrity, respect, teaming, energy and enthusiasm, and the courage to lead. In interviews, that translates into evidence that you act with integrity (critical in a profession built on trust), collaborate genuinely, take initiative, and put clients first. The firm publicly emphasizes hiring diverse backgrounds and a growth mindset, so demonstrating curiosity and willingness to learn lands well.

Practically, interviewers are listening for teamwork, client focus, integrity, resilience under deadline pressure, and — for consulting — structured problem-solving.

## How to prepare

- **Build a story bank first.** Prepare six to eight STAR-structured stories covering teamwork, leadership, deadline pressure, difficult stakeholders, and failure. Our [STAR method examples](/alex/blog/star-method-examples/) show exactly how to shape them.
- **Prepare a specific "why EY."** Know what your target service line actually does and reference it. Generic Big Four answers are a common way to lose points.
- **Prep for your service line's technical or case layer.** Consulting: practice cases (market sizing, profitability, structuring). Assurance/tax: review core accounting, audit, or tax concepts. Technology: rehearse role-specific technical questions.
- **Practice for assessments and video rounds.** Situational-judgment tests reward choosing collaborative, client-first responses; video answers reward concise, structured delivery.

### A worked STAR answer

**Question: "Tell me about a time you managed competing deadlines."**

- **Situation:** During an internship, three deliverables for different managers all landed in the same week.
- **Task:** I had to deliver all three at quality without dropping a ball or burning out.
- **Action:** I mapped each task's true deadline and effort, confirmed priorities with each manager rather than guessing, and front-loaded the most complex piece. When one task's scope grew, I flagged it early and renegotiated the timeline instead of silently slipping.
- **Result:** All three shipped on time, and one manager specifically noted that I'd communicated proactively rather than going quiet under pressure. I learned that managing expectations early is half of managing deadlines.

## FAQ

**How long does the EY interview process take?**
It varies by service line and region. Campus processes follow a seasonal calendar; experienced hires commonly report a few weeks across assessments and interviews.

**Does EY use online assessments?**
Many early-career processes commonly report gamified, situational-judgment, or reasoning assessments early in the pipeline. Prepare for the specific format your application uses.

**Are EY interviews case-based?**
Consulting and strategy roles commonly report case interviews. Assurance, tax, and technology roles lean more toward behavioral fit plus role-specific technical questions.

**What values does EY interview for?**
Publicly, EY emphasizes integrity, respect, teaming, enthusiasm, and the courage to lead, under its "building a better working world" purpose. Prepare stories that demonstrate these clearly.
