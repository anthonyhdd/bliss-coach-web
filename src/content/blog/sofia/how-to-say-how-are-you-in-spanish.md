The most common way to say "how are you" in Spanish is **¿Cómo estás?** (informal) or **¿Cómo está usted?** (formal). Both come from the verb *estar* (to be), and the only difference is how well you know the person — you use *estás* with friends, family, and people your age, and *está usted* with strangers, elders, or anyone you want to show respect to.

That covers 90% of situations. But Spanish is rich with warmer, more casual, and regionally flavored ways to ask the same thing, and knowing a few will instantly make you sound less like a textbook. Here's the full toolkit.

## The Two Essentials: Formal vs. Informal

Spanish splits "you" into an informal *tú* and a formal *usted*, and your greeting follows suit.

| Situation | Say this | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Friend, sibling, kid, peer | **¿Cómo estás?** | How are you? (informal) |
| Stranger, elder, boss, client | **¿Cómo está usted?** | How are you? (formal) |
| A group of people | **¿Cómo están?** | How are you all? |

A quick tip on the accent: *estás* (with the accent on the *á*) is "you are." Drop the accent and *estas* means "these" — so the tilde carries real meaning, just like in **¿Cómo estás?**

In Mexico, people are generally warm and polite, so when in doubt with someone older or in a shop, lean formal with **¿Cómo está usted?** — it's never wrong to be respectful.

## The Casual Favorites

Beyond the textbook version, these are what you'll actually hear on the street. They're all informal and friendly.

- **¿Qué tal?** — "How's it going?" Ultra-common, works in almost any casual setting, and can also mean "what's up."
- **¿Cómo te va?** — "How's it going for you?" / "How are things?" A lovely, natural everyday option.
- **¿Qué onda?** — "What's up?" This is *very* Mexican. *Onda* literally means "wave," and this phrase is the Mexican equivalent of "what's up, dude?" Use it with friends.
- **¿Qué pasó?** — Literally "what happened?" but used as a casual greeting in Mexico, roughly "hey, what's up?"
- **¿Qué haces?** — "What are you doing?" / "Whatcha up to?"
- **¿Cómo andas?** — "How are you getting on?" A bit more colloquial and warm.

If you want to sound like you actually live in Mexico, **¿Qué onda?** is your golden phrase. Pair it with a name — **¿Qué onda, güey?** (what's up, dude?) — and you're speaking real chilango Spanish.

## Warmer and Regional Variations

Once the basics feel comfortable, these add personality and show you're paying attention to the person in front of you.

- **¿Cómo has estado?** — "How have you been?" Perfect when you haven't seen someone in a while.
- **¿Todo bien?** — "Everything good?" A quick, friendly check-in.
- **¿Cómo va todo?** — "How's everything going?"
- **¿Qué me cuentas?** — "What's new with you?" / "What can you tell me?" This one invites a real answer rather than a reflexive "fine."
- **¿Cómo amaneciste?** — literally "how did you wake up?", a warm morning greeting common in Mexico, roughly "how are you this morning?"

That last one, **¿Cómo amaneciste?**, is a small detail that instantly signals you know Mexican Spanish — it's the kind of phrase a *tía* (aunt) uses over breakfast. Layering these in, rather than repeating *¿Cómo estás?* every single time, is exactly what makes your Spanish feel alive rather than memorized. Even switching between **¿Qué tal?** in the morning and **¿Cómo va todo?** in the afternoon shows a comfort with the language that native speakers pick up on right away.

## How to Answer "How Are You"

Getting asked is only half of it — you need a reply. Here are natural answers, from great to not-so-great.

| Spanish | English |
|---|---|
| **Muy bien, gracias.** | Very well, thanks. |
| **Bien, ¿y tú?** | Good, and you? |
| **Todo bien.** | All good. |
| **Aquí, tranquilo.** | Just here, chilling / relaxed. |
| **Más o menos.** | So-so. |
| **Ahí voy.** | Getting by / hanging in there. |
| **La verdad, un poco cansado/a.** | Honestly, a bit tired. |

Two Mexican gems worth adopting:

- **Todo chido** — "All cool / all good." *Chido* is Mexican slang for cool.
- **Ahí la llevo** — literally "I'm carrying it there," meaning "I'm getting by."

Always remember to bounce the question back with **¿y tú?** (and you?) or **¿y usted?** in formal settings. Not returning the question can come across as a little cold.

## Putting It Together: A Real Exchange

Here's how a friendly Mexican greeting actually flows:

> — **¡Hola! ¿Qué onda? ¿Cómo estás?** (Hi! What's up? How are you?)
> — **Todo bien, gracias. ¿Y tú?** (All good, thanks. And you?)
> — **Muy bien también.** (Very well too.)

And a more formal version, say, walking into a shop:

> — **Buenos días. ¿Cómo está usted?** (Good morning. How are you?)
> — **Bien, gracias. ¿En qué le puedo ayudar?** (Well, thanks. How can I help you?)

Notice how *usted* pulls the whole exchange into a respectful register. In shops, with taxi drivers, or meeting someone's grandmother, this is the safe, courteous choice.

## A Note on Pronunciation

- **¿Cómo estás?** → KOH-moh ess-TAHSS
- **¿Qué tal?** → keh TAHL
- **¿Qué onda?** → keh OHN-dah

The upside-down question mark **¿** at the start isn't optional in writing — Spanish opens every question with it. In speech, just let your voice rise at the end like you would in English.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Using *cómo estás* when you mean *cómo eres*.** *Estar* is for temporary states (how you're feeling right now), while *ser* is for permanent traits. "How are you?" as a greeting is always *estar*. Asking *¿Cómo eres?* means "What are you like?" as a person — a totally different question.

**Forgetting the register.** Calling your new boss *tú* with **¿Cómo estás?** on day one can feel over-familiar in formal Mexican settings. When unsure, start with *usted* and let them invite you to *tú*.

**Dropping the accent.** *Estás* needs its accent. Without it, you've written "these."

**Answering and not asking back.** A quick **¿y tú?** keeps the conversation warm and moving.

## FAQ

### What is the most common way to say "how are you" in Spanish?

**¿Cómo estás?** is the most common informal version, and **¿Cómo está usted?** is the formal one. In casual Mexican speech, **¿Qué tal?** and **¿Qué onda?** are also everywhere. All are correct — you just match the formality to the person.

### Is it "cómo estás" or "cómo eres" for "how are you"?

Use **¿Cómo estás?** with *estar*. It asks how you're feeling or doing right now. **¿Cómo eres?** uses *ser* and asks what you're like as a person (your personality or appearance), which is a different question entirely.

### What does "¿Qué onda?" mean?

**¿Qué onda?** is Mexican slang for "what's up?" or "how's it going?" *Onda* literally means "wave," and the phrase is friendly and very casual. Use it with friends and peers, not with your boss or an elder.

### How do I answer "¿Cómo estás?"

The safe, natural answer is **Bien, gracias. ¿Y tú?** (Good, thanks. And you?). For something warmer and more Mexican, try **Todo chido** (all cool) or **Ahí la llevo** (getting by). Always toss the question back with **¿y tú?** or **¿y usted?**.

### Should I use "tú" or "usted" in Mexico?

Use **usted** (formal) with strangers, elders, service workers, and in professional settings — Mexicans value politeness. Use **tú** (informal) with friends, family, kids, and peers. When in doubt, start formal; it's never rude to be respectful.
